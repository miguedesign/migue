Through the internet and social networks, Credilikeme was launched in 2011, a venture with the idea of lending money to people with friends who could validate and trust the debtor's responsibility. Over time, Credilikeme called players to its users, who received badges and reward points, credis, which could be used to cover loan payments.

In 2019, the Credilikeme team approached to collaborate with 23 Design to improve their player experience, rethink the app architecture and refresh user interface and web landing.

Challenge

Our mission was to create a new user experience where players could obtain a loan easily with an ecosystem of visual elements to guide them along the way.

Wouldn't it be great to motivate them to improve their credit score through a game?

From the first meeting with the Credilikeme team, we focused on understanding what the users' pains were, what they were looking for in the app redesign and what new features they wanted to add.

As part of our research, we applied for credits ourselves to better understand the player journey and identify the app's strengths and weaknesses.

Design Process

One of the first tasks was to create an Experience Map to identify users' feelings and to know where players should be rewarded (for example, when making their first payment). Also, as part of the information architecture, we collected all Credilikeme's information available online and internally, organized it and cleaned it up to give a new order to its entire universe.

At the same time, we discovered that the player's experience was confusing in the operations to be done, interest, payment dates and rewards. After several workshops, cups of coffee and many post-its, we finally understood that if our user was the hero of the story, his mission was to pay off the credit.

So we centered the experience on this idea of the player as the protagonist of the story, based on the gamification framework of Yu-Kai Chou: Octalysis, where the player would do paid missions and special missions that would give him credits as a reward. In addition, we added a reward system for each completed paid mission. These rewards (points)would serve the player to qualify and level up.

Let's do it!

Having the solid foundations of the game (app) we redefined the flows; some were adapted and most would be new flows. After hours or days drawing wireframes and checking if they made sense, we debugged and iterated until we were convinced.

Detail design

Moving pixels in detail to make a difference.

Once the flows were validated, we moved on to the visual style (focus time for me) of the new experience. We wanted to move away from the traditional style of a financial app and make it more like a game where advancing and completing missions would take you to the next level.

Being a new visual style, we conducted different collaboration sessions with Credilikeme's product team to gather feedback and understand the legal, commercial and engineering constraints.

Details, clarity and delightful feedback

It's the details that really make an application stand out and adding mobile elements brings it to life without drawing all the attention to itself.

So microinteractions were a resource we implemented to provide delightful feedback, visually celebrating small achievements, e.g. leveling up, waiting for the interface to finish loading or completing certain tasks.

We used different levels of prototype fidelity in the design process with clear objectives, such as navigating flows, completing a task, or showing the engineering team what the functionality should look like. The use of prototypes was key to validate hypotheses and make decisions throughout the development of the project.

/// CONCLUSION

We know that many people are now enjoying the journey of increasing their credit score.
